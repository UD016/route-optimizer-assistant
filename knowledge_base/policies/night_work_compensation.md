## Night Work Compensation

### Purpose

This policy explains how to handle compensation when a technician works past midnight and finishes in the early morning hours. It also defines the required rest period, scheduling restrictions, and the administration process for entering the compensation correctly.

### Policy Overview

When a technician finishes work during after-hours, the technician must receive a minimum of **8 hours of rest** before returning to work.

This policy exists for two main reasons:

1. **Safety and logbook reset requirements**  
   If a technician finishes work at **3:00 a.m.**, they must have an **8-hour rest period** before returning to work. In that example, the earliest return to work would be **11:00 a.m.**

   In the past, technicians sometimes slept only a few hours before returning to work. This was not ideal from a safety standpoint. Under this policy, the technician may receive up to **4 hours of paid time** the following morning so they can rest while still completing the required 8-hour reset.

2. **Incentive for after-hours work**  
   This policy also serves as an incentive to encourage technicians to accept after-hours calls, while recognizing the additional demands of working outside normal hours.

### Rest Requirement

When finishing work during after-hours, technicians must receive:

- Minimum **8 hours rest** before returning to work

Example:

- Work completed: **3:00 a.m.**
- Earliest return to work: **11:00 a.m.**

### Compensation Rule

If the rest period overlaps with the normal morning schedule, the technician may be paid for up to **4 hours** of morning rest time.

Example:

- Technician finishes work at **3:00 a.m.**
- Required 8-hour reset ends at **11:00 a.m.**
- Technician may be paid from **7:00 a.m. to 11:00 a.m.** while remaining at home
- After **11:00 a.m.**, the technician may be scheduled for work as normal

Use the appropriate work order number listed on the **monthly internal Ottawa work order sheet** when recording this compensation.

### Technician Instructions

On the day you receive this compensation:

- Record the morning hours you remained at home for your required rest period
- Maximum payable rest time is **4 hours**
- Use the work order number from the monthly Ottawa internal work order sheet
- Enter the timecard correctly according to branch process

### Administration Responsibilities

Administration must ensure the following:

- Do not schedule a technician before they have completed their required **8-hour rest period** following an after-hours call
- When entering technician time, use the work order identified as **"4 Hour Night Shift Incentive Pay"**
- Once the technician's rest period has ended, they may be scheduled for work as normal for the remainder of the day

### Work Order Reference

Use:

```text
4 Hour Night Shift Incentive Pay
```

when applicable.

### Effective Date

This policy is effective immediately.
