## Purpose

This document provides the standard hourly billing rates for Ottawa Mobile Generator Field Service.

It is intended to help Service Coordinators determine the appropriate labor rate, mileage charge, and minimum billing requirements when creating quotations and work orders.

> **Note**
>
> This document is for **internal use only**.
>
> Rates may change without notice.

---

# Effective Date

- Effective: **January 1, 2026**

---

# Standard Business Hours

## Monday to Friday (Normal Business Days)

| Time | Generator Size | Hourly Rate | BMS Rate Type |
|------|----------------|------------:|---------------|
| 08:00 – 17:00 | 0–250 kW | **$215.07/hr** | ISRT – Tier A |
| 08:00 – 17:00 | 251–999 kW (Default) | **$221.10/hr** | ISRT – Tier B |
| 08:00 – 17:00 | 1000 kW and above | **$233.16/hr** | ISRT – Tier C |

---

# Mileage

Mileage is billed at:

| Charge | Rate |
|---------|-----:|
| Mileage | **$3.25/km** |

Applies at all times.

---

# Minimum Billing Requirement

A minimum of **4 hours of labor** at the applicable labor rate must be billed whenever a technician is dispatched.

This applies to:

- Scheduled service calls
- Emergency service calls

---

# Coordinator Responsibilities

Before creating a quotation or work order:

- Determine the generator size to select the correct labor tier.
- Verify whether after-hours, weekend, or holiday rates apply.
- Apply the appropriate BMS labor type.
- Apply the correct mileage charge.
- Ensure the minimum four-hour labor charge is respected whenever a technician is dispatched.

---

# Common Mistakes

## Incorrect Generator Tier

Always verify the generator size before selecting the labor tier.

---

## Missing Labor Override

After-hours, weekend, and holiday work requires the appropriate **Labor Override** rate.

---

## Incorrect Mileage

Always apply the current Ottawa mileage rate when applicable.

---

## Minimum Labor Not Applied

Every technician dispatch must include a minimum of **4 billable labor hours** at the applicable rate.
